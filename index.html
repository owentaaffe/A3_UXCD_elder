Product Definition

    Elder is a social application which allows you to stay connected to family and friends by creating a dynamic online memory box containing photos, videos and audio. It can be used by people of all ages, but has been designed primarily with the older generation in mind. It includes a collaborative feature which allows you to share a link with others, so that they can upload content on your behalf.

Persona

Personal Details

  • Retired over 10 years.

  • Lives with his wife of 50 years .

  • Has 2 children and 6 grandchildren all living away from home.

  • Busy social life.

  • Member of the Active Retirement Group, MensShed and Bowling Club.

  • Volunteers at the weekend with Parkrun Ireland.

Goals

  • Wants to stay in touch with family and friends.

  • Needs to be able to view photos and videos of his grandchildren.

  • Wants to share photos and videos with his children and friends.

  • Collect memories from the past.

Context

Liam is a retired married man living in South County Dublin. His children and grandchildren live away from home. He has a smartphone and uses it to access the Internet, mostly for WhatsApp messages and to read the paper.

Liam is nervous about using the internet but at the same time doesn’t want to get left behind.

He desperately wants to stay connected to family and friends.

Scenario

Liam loves hurling. He is a loyal supporter of Tipperary hurling team and played for them himself as a young man.

His 8 year old grandson Sean is currently living in Pennsylvania with Liam's son Patrick. Sean is a member of the local Irish-American GAA club and after a year of regular training they have their first competitive match. Liam would dearly love to be standing on the sidelines cheering on his grandson but unfortunately Liam can't make that trip.

He has asked his son Patrick to video the match and send some photos to him via the Elder App so he can see the match for himself. Liam plans to watch the match over and over again. He also plans to bring his phone to the Active Retirement Group when they meet up next, so he can show his friends his grandson playing a hurling match in America.

Application
Elder
Memory Box
Photo Gallery
Primary Actor:
Secondary User (Family member or friend)
Description:
A family member uploads photos to their elderly relative’s photo gallery
Trigger
The primary user (elderly person) sends an email request to a family member containing a clickable share link to the photo gallery in the application
Preconditions:
  1. Family member receives an email containing a shared link to the application from the primary user
  2. The email contains a message from the primary user requesting that they upload some photos to their gallery
  3. Family member clicks the link provided to access the application
  4. Family member’s identity is authenticated
  5. Family member can access the application as a guest
Postconditions:
  1. Family member successfully uploads photos to the gallery
  2. Family member can view the photos they uploaded
  3. Elderly relative or primary user can view the uploaded photos
Priority:
High
Basic Flow:
  1. The user accesses the photo gallery
  2. T
    he user selects from the following options - upload photo and view photo
  3. The system requests access to the user’s photo folder on their mobile device
  4. The user grants access to the photo folder
  5. The user chooses the photo(s) and selects upload option
  6. The system uploads the photo file(s)
  7. The user can view the uploaded photo(s)
  8. The system saves the file(s) and presents the updated view of the gallery
Alternative Courses:
AC1 User does not grant access to their photo folder
  1. Returns user to main photo gallery page
AC2 User selects photo file which is too large
  1. System returns error message stating files must be below 2MB - provides option to upload a different image or crop existing image
  2. User selects upload new image
Extensions
[Ref: Step 7 on Basic Flow]
  1. The user selects ‘Delete’
  2. The system returns an alert message ‘Are you sure?’ and provides clickable options for ‘Yes’ and ‘No’
  3. The user clicks ‘Yes’
  4. The system deletes the photo files and returns to step 5
